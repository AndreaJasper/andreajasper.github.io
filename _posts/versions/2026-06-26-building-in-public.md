Building in public is uncomfortable.

In a **previous chapter** of my career, putting myself out there came naturally. Building relationships, networking, and meeting new people were all part of the job. I rarely thought twice about it. 

Lately, I’ve been feeling the anxiety of this blog, of being public with what I’m learning and where I’m struggling. I’ve spent years talking to executives, managing clients, running meetings, so why does this feel different?

Because this blog is personal instead of professional. That’s why these thoughts show up.

*Am I good enough?*

*Will people think I don’t know what I’m doing?*

*Does documenting the learning process make me look inexperienced?*

Logically, I know these thoughts aren’t helpful, but I can’t help feeling that I’m being judged by friends, family, old coworkers, recruiters, and mostly, myself. I fear that people will read these posts and think “she has no idea what she's doing”. 

There are no guardrails, just my personal experiences. It’s scary. I keep wondering if someone I used to work with is going to read this.

And yet…

Here I am. 

Still writing. 

Still publishing.

Still sharing what I’m learning, even when it feels uncomfortable.

I think I’ve been approaching this blog backwards. I’ve been writing for imaginary readers instead of myself.

Maybe that’s why building in public felt so uncomfortable.

I’m worried about whether strangers think I’m qualified, when the whole point of this blog was to document becoming qualified.

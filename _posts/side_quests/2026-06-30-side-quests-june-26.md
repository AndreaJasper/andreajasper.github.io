**Video Games**
**Elden Ring:** Finally became Elden Lord after owning the game for two years. It was a great challenge, and I thoroughly enjoyed the DLC. Except Promised Consort Radahn…
**No Man’s Sky:** Circled back to my favorite sandbox game just in time for a new expedition. Tinkering with a new corvette ship build and creating tiny bases.
**Pragmata:** I recently started this and Diana is my favorite part. I’ll have more to report next month when I’ve finished.

**TV**
**The Boys:** Season 5 excellent show. I felt the ending was okay. I think I was looking for something grittier.
**Friends and Neighbors:** What in the world lol. I really enjoy this show and can’t wait for the next season.
**The Terminal List:** Loving this show so far. Lots of action and intrigue. 

**Movies**
**Avatar:** Fire and Ash: I LOVED this movie. It felt almost like coming home. Very reminiscent of the first movie. 
**Project Hail Mary:** I now understand the hype around this movie.
**A Tale of Two Sisters:** I love psychological horror and this movie absolutely delivered. Highly recommend if you’re looking for something twisty 

**Books**
**Hidden Pictures:** Started and finished this with my tiny book club consisting of myself, my sister and her best friend. It was our “book of the month” and I really enjoyed it. Thrillers have been my top genre for the last two years, not surprised it might be the top category this year as well.
**House of Leaves:** I’ve started this, but haven’t gotten far. I’ve been busy with other projects and work. The book itself is amazing. Definitely something you need to hold in your hands to experience.
**Occult Feminism:** The Secret History of Women’s Liberation: I read this one on a recommendation, and it’s an interesting read. I’m still sitting with it, but it did make me think a lot about what we were taught about the feminist movement.

Apparently this month was all about science fiction and unsettling stories.
